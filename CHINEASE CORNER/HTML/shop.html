Chinese Foods

Dim Sum

Dim sum is a style of Chinese cuisine that originated in the Guangdong province in southern China and has become popular all over the world. It is typically served as small bite-sized portions of food served in steamer baskets or small plates and is meant to be shared among a group of people.

cantonese chicken soup

The result is a clear and flavorful soup with a delicate yet robust taste, and tender pieces of chicken that are both nourishing and satisfying. The soup is often served as a comforting and restorative meal, especially when someone is feeling unwell or has a cold.

hakka noddles

The noodles are typically cooked al dente and are stir-fried in a wok with vegetables such as bell peppers, onions, and carrots, as well as meats like chicken, beef, or tofu. A flavorful sauce, made with ingredients like soy sauce, oyster sauce, and hoisin sauce, is then added to the noodles to bring all the flavors together.

peri-peri chicken

The key ingredient in Peri-Peri Chicken is the Peri-Peri chili, which is a small and fiery chili pepper native to Africa. The chili is combined with other spices, such as garlic, onion, lemon juice, and paprika, to create a flavorful marinade that infuses the chicken with heat and depth of flavor

quick noodles

Quick Noodles are typically packaged in a cup or bowl, along with a flavor packet, and they can be prepared in just a few minutes by adding hot water and allowing the noodles to soak up the flavor. They come in a wide range of flavors, including chicken, beef, shrimp, and various types of Asian-style soups.

vegetable fried rice

The dish is typically prepared in a wok or large frying pan, with the ingredients being stir-fried together over high heat until the rice is evenly coated and the vegetables are tender yet still crisp. The seasonings used can vary, but commonly used ingredients include soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and ginger.

spring roll

The filling for Spring Rolls can vary greatly, but common ingredients include cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, and bean sprouts, as well as meats like pork, chicken, or shrimp. The filling is wrapped in a thin wrapper made from a mixture of flour and water, which is then fried until crispy.

crab rangoon

The filling for Crab Rangoon typically consists of crab meat, cream cheese, and a mixture of seasonings such as green onions, garlic, and soy sauce. The filling is spooned into wonton wrappers, which are then folded into a triangle shape and fried until crispy.

Honey Shrimp

The sauce for Honey Shrimp is typically made by combining honey, soy sauce, ketchup, and cornstarch, which is then heated until thick and sticky. The shrimp are then coated in the sauce and fried until crispy and golden.
